Mizuno Case study

Mizuno dashboard

The Power-BI Challenge by the Mizuno Corp :-

Mizuno Inc. proposed a sales dashboard that would highlight various key insights. The dashboard's accuracy had to be precise down to the last cent, and it had to reflect and analyse more than 4 Million + Volume data. The dataset available in the ODDO system was in a complete disarray and had to be rectified . Drill down Hierarchy , Automatic refresh and periodic automatic sharing were the key features required for the corporation.

Solution :- Techneith’s Power-BI Dashboard

At Technith, we began the process by designing the dashboard's design and ensuring that its user interface (UI) is both very intuitive for users and pleasant on all fronts. We provided a direct connection between Odoo and Power BI using our Odoo Power BI direct connector (link) . With the use of this functionality, we integrated the data and were able to organize the disarrayed data. We used Sale.order, Sale.order.line , product.template, Stock.picking.value & many other major tables that had over 3+ million records.

The dashboard provided to Mizuno featured a single-page visualisation with numerous views and different KPIs with the built in functionality of Drill - Down feature that highlighted items such as Net sales, Cost of goods sold (COGS), Net margin, top-selling product, date slicers, and many more. Although there were more than 3+ million entries in the data being fetched for the Mizuno dashboard, the efficient Techenieth dashboard made sure that it only took 5 to 10 minutes to dynamically refresh the data in a real-time scenario.

Clients can cross-check their report to the last detail using the drill down functionality. When it came to Mizuno, the bar graph showed the product that sold the most over the course of the year. By drilling down, the customer could see which quarter was the most profitable, which month was the most profitable, and which day of the month was the most profitable.

The total sales by amount with respect to the product category and customer category have been shown in two ways one in the form of a bar chart in descending order and on with respect to a tabular table format , both the KPI’s have an Amazing drill down feature which allows user to to examine the the data source from starch .

The description of the KPIs which are visible in the dashboard image can be understood below :-

  • ➤ Total Sales : It reflects the total sales completed by the firm in a given time interval.
  • ➤ Margin : Margin is the amount of Accounting profit a company has left over after paying all its expenses.
  • ➤ COGS: Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) also known as “Cost of Sales” is the cost of acquiring or manufacturing the products that a company sells during a period.
